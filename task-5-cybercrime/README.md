# Task 5-Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide
While looking into phishing, I was most surprised by how realistic fake emails and websites can appear. Attackers frequently imitate logos and language so effectively that even careful students might be deceived by them. I came to understand that paying attention to minor details, such as verifying the sender's address or the correct spelling of a URL, can greatly help in identifying fraudulent activity. Personally, one habit I plan to alter is opening links directly from emails. Instead, I will always manually enter the official website address or use bookmarked links. This straightforward action lowers the chance of being sent to a counterfeit website. The study showed me that preventing cybercrime relies more on regularly practicing safe habits than on using sophisticated tools.
